Go Workshop

GopherConIndia 2016 is proud to work with Ardan Labs to bring to you a full day of the Hardcore Go workshop on Thursday, 18th Feb. 2016, a day before the actual GopherConIndia conference.

This is a 1 day class for any level developer who has some experience with other programming languages and wants to learn Go. This class provides an intensive and idiomatic view of the language. We focus on both the specification and implementation of the language, including topics ranging from language syntax, Go's type system, concurrency, channels and more. We believe this class is perfect for anyone who wants a jump start in learning Go or who wants a more thorough understanding of the language and its internals. We would love to have you attend this paid workshop. Lunch and snacks will be provided.

Note: The proceeds of the Go workshop will go towards part-funding the conference.

A total of only 25 participants for the Go workshop.

Note: If you puchase a workshop ticket and have already purchased a conference ticket before, we shall refund you the amount paid for the conference ticket.

Cory LaNou who works at @influxDB in an interview with GopherConIndia, says that the best way to get a real jump on the Go language is to either attend a Go workshop, meetup, or find somebody that is willing to pair with you who is already experienced with Go.

Who is it for?
  • Programming
    • Studied Computer Science in school or has a minimum of two years of experience programming full time professionally.
    • Familiar with structural and object oriented programming styles.
    • Has worked with arrays, lists, queues and stacks.
    • Understands processes, threads and synchronization at a high level.
    • Operating Systems
      • Has worked with a command shell.
      • Knows how to maneuver around the file system.
      • Understands what environment variables are.
What do you need?

Mac, Windows, or Linux-based Laptop

Go runs great in all these environments, so any laptop you're comfortable using will do. Get your laptop to the workshop. We'll help you get everything installed on your system so you'll learn in the same environment where you'll actually write your own Go programs.

Up-to-date Web Browser

We designed the online experience to take advantage of some of the latest web standards, so you'll need the latest version of Safari, Chrome, Firefox, or IE.

Who teaches this course?

William Kennedy is a managing partner at Ardan Studios in Miami, FL, a mobile and web app development company. He is also a co-author of the book Go In Action, the author of the blog GoingGo.Net and the organizer for the Go and MongoDB Meetups in Miami. He is focused on Go education and through his new venture GoingoGo Training, he continues the cause by working with individuals and groups who want to take their Go knowledge and coding skills to the next level.

What will you learn?

We take a tour of the language. We learn the keywords, built-in functions and syntax. We also explore what is idiomatic and how the language is very orthogonal. This includes following the community standards for coding and style.
